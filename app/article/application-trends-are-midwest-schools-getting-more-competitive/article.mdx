## Application growth in the Midwest

Across major Midwest universities, application volume has steadily increased from 2021 to 2024. This trend reflects both expanding national visibility and the continued effects of test-optional policies, which lowered barriers to applying at highly selective institutions.

The University of Michigan shows the strongest absolute growth, increasing by more than 10,000 applications over this period. Northwestern University follows a similar upward trajectory, though at a smaller scale due to its already highly selective applicant pool.

Together, these patterns suggest that Midwest flagship and elite private institutions are experiencing sustained increases in demand rather than temporary pandemic-era spikes.

## Growth beyond elite institutions

Application growth in the Midwest is not limited to elite private universities. While schools like Northwestern and the University of Chicago continue to see steady increases, flagship public universities such as the University of Michigan and the University of Wisconsin–Madison are driving some of the strongest gains. Even large public institutions like Indiana University Bloomington show meaningful growth, suggesting that rising application volume is a broader regional trend rather than a phenomenon concentrated among highly selective schools.
