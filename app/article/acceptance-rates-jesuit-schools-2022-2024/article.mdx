## What changed between 2022 and 2024?

Across these five schools, selectivity varies widely—and so does how it’s moving over time. Georgetown is consistently the most selective in this set (about 12–13% across all three years), while Fordham admits a majority of applicants (about 54–59%).

The biggest shift is at the College of the Holy Cross, where acceptance rate fell from 36.35% in 2022 to 17.61% in 2024. That’s a drop of 18.74 percentage points over two years.

## A quick reading of each school

- **Georgetown University**: Most selective in this group, staying near 13% each year.
- **Boston College**: Stable in the mid-teens, dipping in 2023 then rebounding slightly in 2024.
- **College of the Holy Cross**: Much more selective by 2024, with a steep two-year drop in acceptance rate.
- **Santa Clara University**: Acceptance rate fell in 2023 then rose in 2024, remaining in the mid-to-high 40s.
- **Fordham University**: Highest acceptance rate here and trending upward from 2022 to 2024.
